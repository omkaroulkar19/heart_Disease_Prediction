Heart Health 101

Welcome to the basics of heart health! Learn what heart disease is, its causes, and how to prevent it.

  • What is Heart Disease? Heart disease refers to conditions affecting the heart, such as coronary artery disease, heart failure, and arrhythmias. It often develops when plaque builds up in the arteries, reducing blood flow to the heart.
  • Major Risk Factors: High cholesterol, high blood pressure, smoking, diabetes, and a sedentary lifestyle can increase your risk.
  • Prevention Tips: Eat a balanced diet, exercise regularly (at least 150 minutes of moderate activity per week), manage stress, and avoid smoking.
Did You Know? Heart disease is the leading cause of death globally, but 80% of cases are preventable with lifestyle changes.

Tutorial: Understanding Blood Pressure

Step 1: What is Blood Pressure?

Blood pressure measures the force of blood against your artery walls. It’s recorded as two numbers: systolic (pressure when the heart beats) over diastolic (pressure when the heart rests).

Normal blood pressure is around 120/80 mmHg.

Step 3: Tips to Manage Blood Pressure

  • Reduce salt intake to less than 2,300 mg/day.
  • Exercise regularly—aim for 30 minutes most days of the week.
  • Manage stress through meditation or deep breathing.

Myth vs. Fact

Let’s debunk some common misconceptions about heart health.

Myth: Heart disease only affects older adults.
Fact: Younger adults can also be at risk, especially with poor lifestyle choices or genetic factors. For example, high cholesterol can start building up in your 20s.
Myth: Women don’t get heart disease.
Fact: Heart disease is a leading cause of death in women. Women may experience different symptoms, like fatigue or nausea, compared to men.
